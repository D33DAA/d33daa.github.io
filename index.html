My First HOTAS™ (29/06/16)

Took a break from Minecraft yesterday due to having a driving lesson in the evening. That and my Thrustmaster HOTAS arrived. I've been playing Elite:Dangerous recently and the jury's verdict is that a HOTAS is the way to go. That and I use my HTC Vive to play, which led to me frantically punching different areas of the keyboard to find the landing gear. I installed the drivers, found a commended binding and then cried over the reference sheet. It's pretty complicated and makes use of multiple 'shift' keys. This is pretty alien to me but I can see that once I get used to it, it will be much easier than the keyboard.

I tried the training missions over again to get a feel for how it works. Free aiming is a bitch, but dogfighting and travelling are a lot more fun. UI Focus is something I'll have to get used to, but from what I can see it'll be actually better than before. Additionally, the binding I had annoyingly had no settings for Galaxy Map, which is easily the most frustrating part of playing in VR. I mapped the camera and "blue circle thing" to the joy stick and my world was changed. Gone are the days taking off the headset to type the name of the system in search and fighting the game to select it as a destination. A solitary tear rolled down my cheek.

That was all I had time for in the evening, but I'm definitely looking forward to using it more. I haven't used it enough to recommend the particular brand, but it is pretty cheap (Around £30 GBP for me) for a HOTAS and was ideal just as an entry level kit. I would however, recommend using any HOTAS for Elite, as it makes the experience a lot more reactive and tangible. Kind of like playing on Expert in DJHero.

Small sidenote, when using the keyboard, your hands in game remain in their idle state, but using a HOTAS will actually match your actions, adding to the feel.

Night Vale Station (27/06/16)

My latest project is a space station in the End. I think it's quite an interesting idea for a village. Naturally, it has high requirements to entry, as you must make your own way to the end before you are allowed to teleport in and out of it. Additionally, I am adding a rule that space helmets (blocks of glass renamed) must be worn with /hat, which is only available to some player ranks. Additionally, you cannot claim land in the End, so I must be able to trust everyone of my residents.

This is mostly inspired by my recent playtime in Elite Dangerous and while I find MC lacking for spacey stuff, I am going to do it anyway. It's also inspired by the Welcome to night Vale Podcast that I've been listening to recently. I hope to incorporate the bizarre humour throught the station while keeping it as advanced and useful as possible. Here's a plan I made in MS Paint for the start of the station:
